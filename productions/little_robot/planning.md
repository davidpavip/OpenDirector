# The Little Robot — Production Planning

## Production Understanding

### Intent

Create an intimate animated short centered on loneliness, trust, and companionship.

### Audience

- Family

### Target Runtime

8–10 minutes

### Themes

- Friendship
- Hope
- Curiosity
- Courage

### Governing Values

- Human imagination comes first.
- Emotion is more important than spectacle.
- Simplicity is preferred over complexity.
- Every scene must advance the relationship.

### Global Constraints

- Family-friendly
- Do not glorify violence
- Preserve character identity
- Use the smallest effective cast
- Use minimal dialogue.
- The relationship must drive every major scene.

### Open Questions

- Final dialogue level
- Final music style
- Whether the village is reached in this film

### Confidence

0.88

# scene-001 — The Discovery

<!-- scene:id=scene-001 -->
<!-- status:decided -->

## Understanding

### Scene Objective

Introduce the lonely boy and establish curiosity about the abandoned robot.

### Emotional Objective

The audience should feel loneliness, curiosity, wonder, and the beginning of hope.

### Story Function

Inciting incident

### Required Story Information

- The boy is alone in the mountain valley.
- The robot appears abandoned and inactive.
- The glowing village is visible in the distance.

### Continuity Requirements

- Introduce the boy's established appearance.
- Introduce the robot before activation.
- Establish sunset lighting.

### Constraints

- No dialogue.
- Slow and emotionally quiet pacing.
- Do not explain the robot's history.

### Assumptions

- The robot is mechanical rather than supernatural.

### Unknowns

- How visibly damaged should the robot appear?

### Risks

- The reveal may be too subtle.
- The opening may feel slow without strong composition.

### Understanding Confidence

0.89

## Ideas

### idea-a — Robot Under the Tree

The boy rests beneath an old tree and notices a metallic hand extending from the grass.

### idea-b — Robot Beside the Path

The boy hears a faint mechanical sound while walking downhill toward the village.

### idea-c — Firefly Reveal

Fireflies gather around a hidden metallic form and guide the boy toward it.

## Critique

### idea-a — Robot Under the Tree

#### Strengths

- Quiet and emotionally focused.
- Simple to stage.
- Supports a controlled visual reveal.

#### Weaknesses

- The reveal could be too subtle.

#### Risks

- The robot may blend into the environment.

### idea-b — Robot Beside the Path

#### Strengths

- Creates immediate curiosity.
- Suggests that the robot may still function.

#### Weaknesses

- Sound becomes more important than the image.

#### Risks

None recorded.

### idea-c — Firefly Reveal

#### Strengths

- Visually memorable.
- Connects the discovery to the environment.

#### Weaknesses

- May make the robot seem magical.

#### Risks

None recorded.

## Decision

### Selected Direction

idea-a, idea-c

### Reasoning

Use the quiet discovery under the tree, while allowing a few fireflies to strengthen the visual reveal without making the robot supernatural.

### Confidence

0.91

# scene-002 — The First Activation

<!-- scene:id=scene-002 -->
<!-- status:ideas -->

## Understanding

### Scene Objective

Show the boy's kindness and establish the robot's loyal but slightly clumsy personality.

### Emotional Objective

Move the audience from uncertainty toward trust.

### Story Function

First relationship beat

### Required Story Information

- The boy attempts a simple repair.
- The robot activates unexpectedly.
- The robot is awkward rather than threatening.

### Continuity Requirements

- Continue from the robot's inactive position.
- Preserve the same location and twilight progression.

### Constraints

- Avoid technical exposition.
- Keep the repair visually understandable.
- The robot must not appear aggressive.

### Assumptions

None.

### Unknowns

- Should the robot speak?
- Should the first repair attempt fail?

### Risks

- The activation could feel too sudden.

### Understanding Confidence

0.81

## Ideas

### idea-a — Silent Activation

The robot's eyes illuminate. It tries to stand but gently falls back into the grass.

### idea-b — One Broken Word

The robot activates and speaks one incomplete word from its forgotten past.

### idea-c — Protective Reflex

The robot activates only when the boy slips near the edge of the hill.
